The Problem

No staff, no scale, no rest.

Every small business owner answers the same 30 questions every single day — in chat, on phone, in DMs — because there's no one else to do it. Hiring is too expensive. Software demands time they don't have. So they stay small, miss leads, work nights, and burn out. The first product to give them affordable staff wins the category.

  • The after-hours blackout. Customers inquire at 10pm. Owner is asleep. Customer moves to a competitor.
  • The repetition tax. The same answers, typed 30 times a day. Kills productivity, kills the joy of the work.
  • The hiring wall. A real employee costs $25K–$40K/year, plus management. Most SMBs can't justify it for the first hire.
  • The software gap. Helpdesk tools (Zendesk, Tidio, Freshchat) cost $50–$500+/month and demand setup, training, and a tech-savvy operator. SMBs don't have any of that.
  • The trust gap. Owners don't adopt dashboards. They live in WhatsApp, Instagram, SMS, email — wherever their customers are. Any solution that asks them to change is a solution that fails.

The Solution

Install the skills you need. Pay for the ones you use.

FMIFY isn't one chatbot. It's an employee — and like any employee, what makes them useful is the set of skills they can do. The hairstylist needs a different skill set than the apothecary needs than the HVAC tech needs. Why Now

Three curves crossed. They didn't cross 24 months ago.

Chat messaging matured into the commerce layer.

WhatsApp Business API costs dropped ~80% since 2022. Instagram DM, SMS, and web chat all converged into production APIs. The infrastructure is finally cheap enough for SMB pricing.

2

AI inference is now cheap enough to be staff.

GPT-4-class intelligence at ~$0.003–$0.010 per 1K tokens. A full customer interaction costs under half a cent. At 100 interactions/day, total inference ~$15/month — viable as a $49/mo product with healthy margin.

3

Chat-first commerce is now the default.

2B+ active WhatsApp users. 47.8M Nigerians on social media. 33M+ US small businesses where 70% of customer questions come through chat. The "will they message?" question is settled.

A fourth tailwind: no incumbent owns this category for SMBs. Helpdesk tools demand setup. Generic chatbots demand customization. Chat-first SMB AI staff is wide open.
